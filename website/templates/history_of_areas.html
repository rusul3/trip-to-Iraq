History of Iraq

Iraq, known as Mesopotamia in ancient times, is considered to be one of the cradles of civilization. The region has a rich history that dates back thousands of years. Here are some key highlights of Iraq's history:

Ancient Mesopotamia

Mesopotamia, meaning "land between the rivers," was home to ancient civilizations such as the Sumerians, Babylonians, and Assyrians. These civilizations developed advanced systems of writing, mathematics, law, and architecture. The famous Code of Hammurabi, one of the earliest known legal codes, was created in Babylon.

Islamic Golden Age

Iraq played a significant role during the Islamic Golden Age, a period of cultural, scientific, and intellectual flourishing in the Islamic world. Scholars in Baghdad made important contributions to various fields, including mathematics, astronomy, medicine, and philosophy. The House of Wisdom in Baghdad became a renowned center of learning.

Ottoman Empire and Modern Iraq

In the 16th century, Iraq came under the rule of the Ottoman Empire and remained under Ottoman control for several centuries. Following the collapse of the empire after World War I, Iraq became a League of Nations mandate under British administration. Iraq gained independence in 1932 but faced political and social challenges throughout the 20th century. The country experienced various conflicts, including the Iran-Iraq War and the Gulf War.

Recent History

Iraq faced significant political changes in the 21st century. The 2003 invasion by a coalition led by the United States resulted in the overthrow of Saddam Hussein's regime. Subsequent years saw efforts to establish stability and democracy in Iraq, although the country faced ongoing challenges, including sectarian tensions and the rise of extremist groups.
